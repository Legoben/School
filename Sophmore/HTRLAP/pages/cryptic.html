Authors Usually Mean More Than What They Say

When writers write such cryptic meanings into their stories they want there readers to really think about it and figure out what the author meant and they want the readers to really enjoy their story they think they will do that if the understand the cryptic messages and they might appreciate more of the authors works.
Reading deeper in the story can show the hidden things about the characters, such as true motives and their hidden agendas. In the other words, foreshadowing.
